Treasure Hunt

  • Round 1
  • Round 2

  • call Contact

Traditional treasure hunt where you decipher our riddles to find the clues hidden in various parts of our campus.

In this event, the participants will be locked inside a room. The room will consist of a set of clues which the team has to decipher in order to break out the room. Simple. It could be related to general chemisty, brain storming puzzles,etc.. The only twist is that if you do not break out of the room in the given time limit, you are in for a big trouble. Come On Heisenbergs, Break Bad!!

  • A team should consist of 3 participants.
  • Each team is provided with a group of clues/riddles which the team must solve in order to find a particular piece of information. Each clue leads to a location within the defined perimeter.
  • Do not open and read the clues prior to the announcement that the hunt has started. Only after a thumbs-up from the organizing committee, the hunt begins.
  • Teams must travel on foot in and around the campus with the exception of those having a physical limitation.
  • Teams must stay together. All three members must be present together at the clue site and please refrain from using mobile phones to communicate which might help you to solve the clues. In case of an emergency he/she can inform the organizing committee and leave.
  • Teams may use smartphones or other internet devices but we do ask you to use it responsibly. Neither the mobile nor the internet would help you anyway. Avoid the usage of whatever strikes in your mind as a loophole to the clues.
  • Teams are kindly requested not to displace, tamper or alter the answers at the clue locations. We have a group of volunteers to keep an eye on all the clue locations. God is watching over all anyway.
  • After solving all the clues, teams are requested to return the answer sheet at the designated end point.
  • The first four entries to finish within the least possible time taken and with the most correct answers progresses to the next final event ‘Breaking Bad’.
  • All the team members should be present while handing over the answer sheet with the exception of those in an emergency (Rule 5).
  • If it is discovered that malpractice had taken place, the respective team could be disqualified.
  • The teams are requested to bring necessary stationery items. Since there will be a lot of walking involved participants are requested to come in comfortable clothing of their choice.
  • Your team will not be asked to do anything illegal or go to places where public access is not granted.

This is a game where one of the participants will be given a placard with a word written on it. The participant must help the other teammate to find the word. The person who is giving the clues is allowed to give a max of 3 clues with each clue not being more than 2 words. The placard will also contain words called the taboo words that the person giving clues musn't use. The teams that find the most number of words at the 2 minutes will move to the next round.

One of the teammates will be given a placard with a question written on it. The participant having the placard will read out the question and the other has to answer it. All this happening only with 7 seconds on the clock.  At end of this game the top 4 teams will move on to the next round.

This is a game where a teammate will be given a placard containing either a process , an element or a thing. This teammate has to help the other to find out the word by only attempting to draw the word in a sheet of paper. Around 6-8 words will be given to a team. And these 6-8 words will describe more than one process. The first team to identify all the words and to decipher the processes the words explain will be announced as the winner.

Saviors Speech

  • Round 1
  • Round 2
  • call Contact

So the game goes about like this… you contestants are in a crisis situation where you are each provided with an element or a compound. On the placard that is provided to thee, three advantages and three disadvantages of the compound will be provided and you will be given a time limit of 1 minute to prepare on your element or compound. After this, your sole purpose is to convince the judges on why they, as a savior, must save you (your allotted compound/element) and how it will benefit us in the future. Individual speaker time is 1.30 minutes and rebuttal questions from the judges and the audience shall be entertained for a period of 30 – 60 seconds. The elements shall be hand-picked by the contestants from a bowl of chits and the placards will then be handed over to the same. Contestants shall be judged based on their speaking skills and the thrust they provide in convincing the audience. The top 6 speakers chosen will move into the second round. May the odds be ever in your favor!!

This round involves a panel discussion on a major crisis situation that causes a multitude of problems for the environment. The shortlisted participants from the first round will have a panel discussion on a topic which will remain anonymous until after the shortlisting of the participants. Each participant will get an individual speaker time of two minutes whenever he or she wishes to speak. Cross questioning is permitted but the speaker will answer only one genuine question (to be decided by the chair). The chair will be allowed to rebuttal and question the speakers. At the end of the panel discussion a consensus must be reached and based on the quality of the discussion, the chair will decide the winner for of both the rounds. In order to enable the contestants to start their research on the topic, a word document shall be uploaded on the official website shortly. Note: The rebuttal session can be extended purely on the basis of factual errors or necessity of discussing the issue at hand. MAY THE BEST SPEAKER WIN!!

SUPERHUMAN CHALLENGE

Create your own superhero (CREATIVE/DESCRIPTIVE round)
The participants will be allowed to come in teams of two each and they will have to choose one placard. This placard will contain one manmade disaster written on it. The participants will be provided with an A4 sheet and pencil/eraser (optional, we could tell them to get this). The pre- requisite requirement for this event will be that the participants will have to get internet pack in their phones. Once the A4 sheet is provided, they will be given approximately 20-25 minutes to browse and create their descriptive story along with a sketch of their hero with the powers they want. Maximum powers they could include in their hero would be 10. The superhero should tackle the disaster given to them in the placard by using its powers. The most creative and imaginary superhero will be shortlisted for the next round. In case the participants fail to get internet, we will have two laptops with wifi connected and the respective team will be given 5-7minutes to gather as much information they want and jot it down. At the end of 25 minutes, the sheets will be collected from the teams and evaluated based on their powers to tackle the situation and their creativity.

Auction of Super Powers (Brain)
This round involves real time auction scenario wherein the shortlisted participants from the first round will be shown a PowerPoint slides. This round will require the use of projector where in the first slide will be divided into two section. the 1st section will contain the list of total disasters which we have taken and the other section will contain a list of additional super powers. Once the shortlisted participants gather, we will give them 100 tokens each team (its tentative yet, we need to finalize on the number of tokens). The slide of the superpower and incidents will be shown to them for 5-10minutes after which the participants will be told to buy maximum 3 super powers in addition to their existing powers. Now, here is where the main game begins. The participants will be judged on the powers chosen by them (as to how many maximum disasters their superhero can handle with the existing and additional superhero powers) and also how economical their superhero is ( i.e.: how many tokens are left at the end of the auction). We will have a live update on the screen while the auction happens ,thus the maximum team per auction will be 12. There can be many auctions depending on the number of participants.
P.S: We are planning for participants as wild card entry in this round. This will be one of the main highlight of this event. We will create a form in Google docs which will be out 3 days before Chemflux’17 and people who register will be sent a disaster and they will be given full freedom to use any editing software and make their own superhero. The participants who send in their entries before the given date and time will be shortlisted and sent to the 2nd round directly.
